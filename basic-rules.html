Basic Rules

Here is a list of the basic rules of volleyball. This is a great place to start if you want to learn more about the sport.

1. Teams: Two teams of six players each.
2. Objective: Score points by grounding the ball on the opponentâ€™s court or when the opponent commits a fault.
3. Serve: A player serves the ball from behind the end line to start the rally.
4. Rotations: Teams rotate clockwise each time they win the serve from the opponent.
5. Touches: Each team is allowed up to three consecutive touches to return the ball over the net.
6. No double hit: A player cannot hit the ball twice in a row.
7. No catch or throw: The ball must be hit cleanly, not caught or thrown.
8. Net: Players cannot touch the net during play.
9. Ball in/out: The ball is in if it lands on the boundary lines; out if it lands outside.
10. Scoring: Matches are usually played best of five sets; sets are played to 25 points.
11. Back-row attack: Back-row players cannot attack the ball in front of the attack line.
12. Blocking: Blocking a serve is not allowed.
13. Faults: Faults include touching the net, crossing the center line, double hits, four touches, or improper serves.
